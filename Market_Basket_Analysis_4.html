def prepare_item_pairs(order_item):
    for order_num, order_key in groupby(order_item, lambda x:x[0]):
        item_list = [item[1] for item in order_key]
        for item_pair in combinations(item_list, 2):
            yield(item_pair)

#CONFIDENCE
#Say we have two items X and Y, confidence measures the percentage of times Y is purchased given that X was purchased.
#Which can be represented by CONFIDENCE{Y|X} = SUPPORT(Y|X)/ SUPPORT(X)

#Let's try to calculate it
'''
SUPPORT X = SUPPORT APPLE
SUPPORT {Y | X} = SUPPORT(BROCOLLI | APPLE)

This ratio SUPPORT ( BROCOLLI | APPLE) / SUPPORT (APPLE) in English means
the ratio of BROCOLLI was bought when Apple was already bought in entire order history
divided by how many times apple was bought in the entire order history.





CONDIFENCE (Y | X) = (4/5)/ (4/5) = 1 = 100%

Confidence values range from 0 to 1, 
where 0 indicates that Y is never purchased when A is purchased, 
and 1 indicates that Y is always purchased whenever A is purchased. 
Which is true in our case, that whenever apple was purchased there was brocolli as well in the list.
Of course we also see in Order 2, brocolli is there but no apple was purchased.


BUT BUT BUT
CONFIDENCE (Y|X) is NOT EQUAL TO CONFIDENCE (X|Y)

CONFIDENCE (X|Y) = SUPPORT (APPLE|BROCOLLI) / SUPPORT (BROCOLLI) = (4/5)/ (5/5) = 80%


So what we can derive is there is 100% chance that
whenever Apple was purchased, Brocolli was purchase every time.

But whenever Brocolli was purchase only 4 out of 5 times Apple was also bought.
That means Confidence is a directional metric.

'''

#Okay so what is LIFT?

'''


order 1: apple, exotic fish, brocolli  
order 2: egg, brocolli, Chocolate  
order 3: apple, egg, brocolli
order 4: apple, egg, Chocolate, brocolli
order 5: apple, brocolli, Pickle



Lift explains, two items are together in a order, is it random or there is a relationship.

Say two items Apple and Eggs has zero relationship and is random to appear together in a order.
That means, Apple appears 80% (4/5) of the time in entire order history
and egg appears 60% (3/5) times in entire order history.
So it means the likelihood of getting both apple and eggs in a order 
is (80%) X (60%) = 48%, and has no relationship to be in the SAME order placed.

Now, we would like to whether this is random or not.
LIFT = 1 means, there is no relationship between APPLE And EGGS.
(i.e: APPLE AND EGGS occur together only by chance)

LIFT > 1, means there is a positive relationship for both items to be together.
(ie:  APPLE and EGGS occur together more often than random)

LIFT < 1, there is a negative relationship between APPLE AND EGGS.
(ie:  APPLE and EGGS occur together less often than random)

Let's solve to find what is the relationship between Apple and Egg to be 
together in same order, is it random or something else.


order 1: apple, exotic fish, brocolli  
order 2: egg, brocolli, Chocolate  
order 3: apple, egg, brocolli
order 4: apple, egg, Chocolate, brocolli
order 5: apple, brocolli, Pickle

So what is the chance of ordering apple  = 4/5 = 80%
What is the chance or ordering eggs = 3/5 = 60%
And how many time apple and eggs are ordered together = 2/5 = 40%

LIFE ( APPLE|EGGS) = SUPPORT(APPLE, EGGS) / {SUPPORT(APPLE) X SUPPORT(EGGS)}

= 40%/ (60% X 80%)
= 0.83 which is less than 1
That means there is a negative relationship between apple and eggs to be in same order.
(ie:  APPLE and EGGS occur together less often than random)

def get_item_pairs(order_item):
    order_item = order_item.reset_index().as_matrix()
    for order_id, order_object in groupby(order_item, lambda x: x[0]):
        item_list = [item[1] for item in order_object]
              
        for item_pair in combinations(item_list, 2):
            yield item_pair
